DeFi

Why am I focused on DeFi?
  • Central banking is a rigged game where the rich and elite can decide how to print money, and who it should go to. Controlling money is the largest control mechanism that elites have outside of war. We need to design new monetary systems outside of central banking that make money an open and fair game to play for all citizens from all countries.
  • As we solve the problem explained above, blockchains simultaneously provide us with open algorithms that will put identity, privacy, knowledge, voting, data, and reputation back in the hands of the people and create a Modern Internet Democracy. Right now we live in a modern internet age, yet we are governed by a 200-year-old democracy. With blockchain, we can turn the Internet from a tool for authoritarian control into a tool for decentralized democracy
Where do I think DeFi is headed?
  • I believe that most DeFi protocols are not fully decentralized, and that we need to better define the categories of protocols, which I have outlined in my blog - Crypto — The Escape Hatch For The Broken Global Financial System.
I propose separating DeFi into two categories immediately:
  • Decentralized Finance (DeFi) - a set of permissionless, public, peer-to-peer financial protocols that are open-source, have no owners, and involve no third parties.
  • Open Finance (OpenFi) - companies or DAOs that offer financial services built entirely upon permissionless, public, peer-to-peer financial protocols.
Furthermore, we need to define completely new monetary systems. DeFi and OpenFi both treat money the exact same way we do in traditional finance, only with different degrees of decentralization.

With new financial technology, we can completely rethink how we treat money. This rethinking of how we use money, and what money is at its core, is what I am most interested in and currently working on. The best example I have seen of this is Collaborative Finance from Informal Systems. I believe CoFi has an excellent shot at redefining how we treat money. If not CoFi, then I am 100% convinced there will be at least one successful project that redefines money in the next decade, and will shape global finance for the next century.
